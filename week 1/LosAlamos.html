Absolute References

Absolute references (sometimes called external links) connect to pages in other domains. In the href attribute of the tag the full URL of the page, image, or other resource you would like to link to is specified. one example might be you quest to list the scientist involved in Los Alamos creating the Atomic Bomb.

  • J.Robert Oppenheimer

  • Hans Bethe

An advantage of absolute references is that you always linked to the most up-to-date information on that page. A disadvantage is that it is up to you to make sure that the links don't change.

Relative References

Relative references (sometimes called internal links) connect to pages in other domains. In the href attribute of the tag only the folder (if any) and filename is specified. Some examples of when you might user relative references would be in your navigation bar or links to your images.
